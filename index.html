The Psychology of Money

Author: Morgan Housel

In this book, author explores explores how emotions, biases, and personal experiences shape our financial decisions. Housel emphasizes that wealth-building relies less on intelligence and more on behavior—such as patience, self-control, and understanding risk. Through engaging anecdotes, he shows why managing money well is often more about mindset than complex strategies, highlighting timeless lessons in humility, flexibility, and the value of simplicity in finance. This book redefines wealth and challenges traditional perspectives on saving, investing, and spending.

Author: Eckhart Tolle

In this book author explores the concept of living fully in the present moment. Tolle argues that many people are trapped in their thoughts about the past or future, which causes stress and unhappiness. By focusing on the "Now," readers can transcend their mental limitations and experience a deeper sense of peace and fulfillment. The book provides practical guidance on how to achieve this mindfulness and embrace the present as a pathway to spiritual awakening...

Thinking, Fast and Slow

Author: Daniel Kahneman

In this book author explores the dual systems that drive our thoughts and decisions: System 1, which is fast, automatic, and emotional, and System 2, which is slow, deliberate, and logical. Kahneman, a Nobel Prize-winning psychologist, examines how these systems shape our judgments and choices, often leading to cognitive biases and errors. Through compelling examples and research, he reveals the complexities of human thinking and offers insights into improving decision-making processes. The book is a profound look at the mind's inner workings and the factors influencing our behavior...
